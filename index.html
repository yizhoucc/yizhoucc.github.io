About

I am a PhD student in the Pitkow Lab at Carnegie Mellon University, working with Xaq Pitkow. My research focuses on inverse rational control (IRC) — understanding the objectives and decision-making processes of agents by observing their behavior.

I am broadly interested in how computational models can explain neural and behavioral data, bridging the gap between machine learning theory and neuroscience experiments. My work combines techniques from reinforcement learning, Bayesian inference, and causal reasoning.

Research

Inverse Rational Control

Recovering reward functions and internal models from observed behavior using the IRC framework, to infer the computational processes underlying neural activity and decision-making.

Active Learning

Designing experiments that efficiently reveal an agent's internal model by adaptively selecting stimuli and task conditions.

Computational Neuroscience

Analyzing neural data with modern statistical and machine learning tools to uncover principles of neural computation.
